What is a Stroke?

A stroke is a serious medical condition that occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted. Immediate treatment is crucial to minimize damage and improve recovery outcomes.

Types of Strokes

  • Ischemic Stroke: Caused by blood clots or narrowed arteries reducing blood flow to the brain.
  • Hemorrhagic Stroke: Caused by ruptured blood vessels bleeding into or around the brain.
  • Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA): Known as a mini-stroke, symptoms last a few minutes to hours without permanent damage.

Key Facts About Stroke

Global Impact

Every year, over 15 million people experience a stroke globally, with 5 million deaths and 5 million disabled survivors.

Prevention is Key

Up to 80% of strokes are preventable through healthy habits like diet, exercise, and managing medical risks.

Act Fast

Immediate action can save lives. Every minute of untreated stroke causes significant brain cell loss.

Recognize the Signs

Use the acronym **FAST** to identify a stroke:

  • F: Face drooping
  • A: Arm weakness
  • S: Speech difficulty
  • T: Time to call emergency services
