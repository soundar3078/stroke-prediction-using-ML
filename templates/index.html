Stroke Prediction

What is Stroke?

A stroke occurs when there is a sudden interruption in the blood supply to the brain. This can be caused by a blockage or rupture of blood vessels, leading to lasting brain damage and potential disability.

Strokes are medical emergencies that require immediate attention. They can occur suddenly, and recognizing the signs early can significantly improve outcomes.

Symptoms of Stroke

  • Sudden numbness or weakness, especially on one side of the body
  • Confusion, trouble speaking or understanding speech
  • Vision problems in one or both eyes
  • Difficulty walking, dizziness, loss of balance or coordination
  • Severe headache with no known cause

Stroke Risk Factors

  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking
  • High cholesterol levels
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Age, gender, and family history
  • Physical inactivity

Prevention Tips

  • Maintain a healthy diet with lots of fruits and vegetables
  • Exercise regularly, aiming for at least 30 minutes of moderate activity most days
  • Monitor your blood pressure and cholesterol levels regularly
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption
  • Stay active, manage stress, and maintain a healthy weight
  • Know your family history of heart disease and stroke

When to Seek Medical Help

If you or someone around you shows signs of a stroke, seek medical help immediately. The faster a stroke is treated, the better the chances of recovery. Call emergency services or go to the hospital right away.
